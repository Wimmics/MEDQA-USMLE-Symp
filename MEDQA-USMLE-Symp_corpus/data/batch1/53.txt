A 76-year-old man comes to the emergency department because of a 12-hour history of fever and left lower quadrant abdominal pain. He has not passed a stool for the past 36 hours. His temperature is 38.3°C (100.9°F). A tender mass is palpable in the left lower quadrant of the abdomen. Stool is negative for occult blood. Laboratory studies show: Hemoglobin 13 g/dL Leukocyte count 17,000/mm3 (84% neutrophils) Platelet count 200,000/mm3 Serum amylase 115 U/L Urinalysis 0 to 1 WBC/hpf An x-ray of the abdomen shows no abnormalities. The most likely diagnosis is an acute episode of which of the following disorders?
